Recall, done right.

The name is shared with a certain much-discussed feature. The architecture is the opposite. On Free and Pro, everything Swoop Recall knows lives in your own browser and never leaves it. When you choose Cloud, your archive is encrypted on your device before a single byte is uploaded — so even the backup is unreadable to anyone but you.

Questions

Good to know.

Do I need Swoop to use Swoop Recall? ▼
Swoop Recall imports the conversation files that Swoop exports, so Swoop is how you get your conversations out of each AI platform. Swoop's free tier covers ChatGPT, Gemini, and Copilot; Swoop Pro adds Claude and Grok. Once you have export files, Swoop Recall archives and searches them.
Where is my data stored? ▼
On Free and Pro, everything lives in your browser's local storage on your own device and is never uploaded. FalqenView never sees it. If you subscribe to Cloud, your archive is encrypted on your device before it leaves your browser, and only the encrypted copy is stored for backup — we cannot read its contents.
What's the difference between Free, Pro, and Cloud? ▼
Free archives up to 10 conversations and is fully featured below that cap. Pro is a one-time purchase that removes the cap for an unlimited local archive on one device. Cloud is an optional yearly subscription that adds encrypted off-device backup across two devices — Cloud requires an active Pro license on the same device, since it builds on Pro rather than standing alone.
What happens when I hit the free 10-conversation limit? ▼
Nothing breaks and nothing is deleted — all 10 stay fully searchable, viewable, and exportable, exactly as before. The cap only affects importing an 11th conversation; everything else about Free keeps working at full strength. If your archive naturally grows past that — which tends to happen once you're regularly using Swoop and Chat Refresh together — Pro removes the cap entirely with a one-time purchase, no subscription.
Can I buy Cloud without Pro? ▼
Cloud sync is available exclusively with an active Pro license — it's built as an enhancement to Pro's unlimited local archive, not a separate standalone tier. Pairing them together gets you an unlimited archive on this device, plus encrypted backup and sync across two devices.
Is Swoop Recall a browser extension? ▼
No. Swoop Recall is a web app you open in any modern browser — no extension to add. You can also install it as its own Mobile Dashboard (WebApp) if you'd like it in its own window with its own icon, but that's entirely optional. Either way, it reads only the files you choose to import into it; it does not access any website or run in the background.
Do I need to install it? ▼
No — Swoop Recall is 100% functional right in your browser, no install required. If you'd like, you also have the option to install it as its own Mobile Dashboard (WebApp), which adds a desktop or home-screen icon and gives you full offline access to your archive. It's entirely optional, and either way, all your data and functionality stay exactly the same.
