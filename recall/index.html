Recall, done right.

The name is shared with a certain much-discussed feature. The architecture is the opposite. On Free and Pro, everything Swoop Recall knows lives in your own browser and never leaves it. When you choose Cloud, your archive is encrypted on your device before a single byte is uploaded — so even the backup is unreadable to anyone but you.

Questions

Good to know.

Do I need Swoop to use Swoop Recall? ▼
Swoop Recall imports the conversation files that Swoop exports, so Swoop is how you get your conversations out of each AI platform. Swoop's free tier covers ChatGPT, Gemini, and Copilot; Swoop Pro adds Claude and Grok. Once you have export files, Swoop Recall archives and searches them.
Where is my data stored? ▼
On Free and Pro, everything lives in your browser's local storage on your own device and is never uploaded. FalqenView never sees it. If you subscribe to Cloud, your archive is encrypted on your device before it leaves your browser, and only the encrypted copy is stored for backup — we cannot read its contents.
What's the difference between Free, Pro, and Cloud? ▼
Free archives up to 10 conversations and is fully featured below that cap. Pro is a one-time purchase that removes the cap for an unlimited local archive, still stored only on your device. Cloud is an optional yearly subscription that adds encrypted off-device backup on top of whatever local tier you're on.
What happens when I hit the free 10-conversation limit? ▼
Your existing conversations stay fully searchable and usable — nothing is deleted. To archive more than 10, upgrade to Swoop Recall Pro for an unlimited local archive.
Is Swoop Recall a browser extension? ▼
No. Swoop Recall is a browser-based web app you open in any modern browser — nothing to install. It reads only the files you choose to import into it; it does not access any website or run in the background.
