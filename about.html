The short version

Built to put you back in control.

FalqenView didn't start with a pitch deck. It started with being fed up, and deciding to build the software I actually wanted to use.

Somewhere along the way, software stopped being simple. Everything wants an account, then another account just to connect to the first one. The free thing is buried in ads. The useful thing nags you to upgrade. The simple thing begs for a donation every time you open it. And most of it quietly ships your data off to a server you'll never see.

I hit it hardest with my own money. Portfolio apps, budgeting dashboards, account aggregators. The pitch never changes: link your accounts, get the full picture. Except the links break, half your accounts aren't supported, and you never really know who's looking at your balances. So I stopped linking and started building.

The rule was simple. Your data stays in your hands, not mine.

VuePoint tracks what you invest. Talon tracks what you owe. Both live inside a Google Sheet you own, with no FalqenView server in the middle and nothing for anyone to breach. Quba came from the same place: most prayer apps are slow, cluttered, plastered with ads, and forever asking for something. Quba stays clean, free, and ad-free, with a UI worth looking at and the features you actually need. The same goes for the free web tools at tools.falqenview.com. Sold or given away, the principle holds: build something genuinely useful, hand people full control, take nothing they didn't choose to give, then get out of the way.
