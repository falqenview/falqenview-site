Quba

The first step is the hardest.
We made it easier.

A gentle, beginner-friendly guide to the five daily prayers — and a compass to face the Kaaba from wherever you are.

Free, always.

A personal gift, offered freely to anyone learning or returning to the basics of prayer. No ads, no accounts, no tracking, no cost. If it's useful to you, that is more than enough.

Why this exists

Most prayer apps are built for people who already know what they're doing. Long menus, dense rulings, assumed knowledge. That leaves new Muslims, younger learners, and anyone coming back to their prayers after time away without a clear, gentle first step.

This app is for those people.

It walks you through each of the five daily prayers — what to say, when to say it, how many rak'ahs, and what every word means in plain language. No prior knowledge assumed.

About the name

In 622 CE, Prophet Muhammad ﷺ arrived in a small settlement called Quba during the Hijra — the migration from Mecca to Medina. Before continuing into the city, he laid the foundation of the first mosque in Islam. The companions built alongside him, stone by stone.

After settling in Medina, the Prophet ﷺ returned to Masjid Quba every Saturday to pray. He said that whoever comes to it in a state of purity and prays there receives a reward equal to that of Umrah. Masjid Quba still stands in Medina today.

This app carries that name simply out of love for what that place represents. Nothing more.

Everything inside

Today's prayer times

The five prayers for today, calculated from your current location, with a gentle countdown to the next — Fajr through Isha, in your local time.

How to pray, prayer by prayer

Each of the five daily prayers shown exactly as it's performed — rak'ahs, what to say, and when to say it. No prior knowledge assumed.

Step-by-step Wudu

Every step of ablution in order, with a progress tracker — what's fard, what's sunnah, written plainly for beginners.

Library of 20 short surahs

Each surah in Arabic, with transliteration and translation — and a one-tap link to Quran.com to listen to the recitation whenever you like.

Live Qibla compass

A real-time compass that points to the Kaaba from wherever you stand — calibrated to your device heading and current location.

Nearest mosques

The five closest masjids to your current location, sorted by distance. Tap any one to open directions straight in Google Maps.

Your madhab, your settings

Choose Hanafi, Shafi'i, Maliki, or Hanbali. Pick your calculation method. Toggle Sunnah Ghair Mu'akkadah per prayer or globally — the app follows how you pray.

Built the way you like it

System, dark, or light theme. Beginner-first notes on common conditions and rulings, with a gentle reminder to consult a trusted local scholar.

A real app, on any device

Install it on your phone or desktop and it runs like any other app — or just open it in any browser. No app store, no download required, no account to create.
