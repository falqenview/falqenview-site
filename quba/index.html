Why this exists

Most prayer apps are built for people who already know what they're doing. Long menus, dense rulings, assumed knowledge. That leaves new Muslims, younger learners, and anyone coming back to their prayers after time away without a clear, gentle first step.

This app is for those people.

It walks you through each of the five daily prayers — what to say, when to say it, how many rak'ahs, and what every word means in plain language. No prior knowledge assumed.

And if you've prayed your whole life, it's still for you. The du'ās, the post-prayer adhkar, the mosque finder, the Qibla compass, the daily prayer times — these are the things we all reach for, no matter how long we've been praying. Beginners find their footing here. Everyone else finds it all in one place.

About the name

In 622 CE, Prophet Muhammad ﷺ arrived in a small settlement called Quba during the Hijra — the migration from Mecca to Medina. Before continuing into the city, he laid the foundation of the first mosque in Islam. The companions built alongside him, stone by stone.

After settling in Medina, the Prophet ﷺ returned to Masjid Quba every Saturday to pray. He said that whoever comes to it in a state of purity and prays there receives a reward equal to that of Umrah. Masjid Quba still stands in Medina today.

This app carries that name simply out of love for what that place represents. Nothing more.

Everything inside

Today's prayer times

The five prayers for today, calculated from your current location, with a gentle countdown to the next — Fajr through Isha, in your local time.

How to pray, prayer by prayer

Each of the five daily prayers shown exactly as it's performed — rak'ahs, what to say, and when to say it. No prior knowledge assumed.

Pray along, step by step

A full-screen guided mode walks you through a whole prayer one move at a time, so the first time you stand to pray, you're never guessing what comes next.

Wudu & prayer tracking

Every step of ablution in order, with a progress tracker showing what's fard and what's sunnah. Then log each prayer as you complete it, and tap back to any day in the past week to record missed prayers.

Surahs, du'ās & adhkar

Twenty short surahs plus the everyday du'ās and Post-Prayer Adhkar you say after salah — each in Arabic with transliteration and translation, and a tap to listen. A built-in tasbih counter on the Prayers tab counts your dhikr down from 33 with a gentle pulse.

Live Qibla compass

A real-time compass that points to the Kaaba from wherever you stand — calibrated to your device heading and current location.

Nearest mosques

The five closest masjids to your current location, sorted by distance. Tap any one to open directions straight in Google Maps.

Your madhab, your settings

Choose Hanafi, Shafi'i, Maliki, or Hanbali, and your prayer times follow. Turn Sunnah Ghair Mu'akkadah (SGM) and Post-Prayer Adhkar (PPA) on or off per prayer or all at once, pick a system, dark, or light theme, and lean on beginner-first notes for common conditions and rulings. The app prays the way you pray.

Read it in your language

Use Quba in English, Arabic, Bengali, Turkish, or Urdu — five of the most widely spoken languages across the Muslim world — with full right-to-left support.

One app, on every device

Add Quba to your phone or computer and it opens like any other app. There's never an app store to wait on, and never an update to chase. Prefer not to install? Just visit quba.falqenview.com in any browser. Adding it to your device is the best way to keep your settings exactly how you like them.
